Change Card Design Wizard

Modernizing online screens for requesting a new debit card design.

Role

UX Designer

Tools

Sketch, Invision, Corporate Insight

Duration

Summer 2021


Overview

This past summer, I worked as a UX design intern on the debit card servicing team at PNC. My core project during this 10 week internship focused on redesigning the step-by-step wizard screens for users to request a new card design on the online banking site. Throughout this internship, I collaborated with developers and project managers to assess design requirements. Due to NDA, most of the final work is under wraps, but I would love to share my process behind the work.



Process

Journey Map

I began by creating a user journey map to understand the current experience of requesting a new card design. My goal was to empathize with users and identify their goals and potential frustrations at each step. Users ultimately want to view relevant design options and easily customize their card.

Pain Points

I focused on some key pain points in this process that could be improved in the redesign. From a user perspective, these elements made the experience more complicated and less intuitive.



Ideation

After initial research, I presented these findings in a group design session to identify areas of opportunity to address these points of frustration. We looked at the bank’s mobile experience as well as competitor banking sites to gather ideas for features. In the end, we came up with several design solutions, which I incorporated in a new user journey map.


New Design Opportunities
  • Drop down to filter design categories and reduce clicks
  • Text describing sports cards’ special benefits links
  • More accessible back and next button
  • Present critical information earlier in the process

Lo-Fidelity Wireframes

After our brainstorming session, I got to work creating lo-fidelity wireframes incorporating initial ideas for the change card design flow. I wanted to create screens that made each step visually clear and simple while still communicating necessary information.




How to design the card selection screen?

Displaying so many card choices

One of the biggest challenges was determining how to organize and display all these cards within a single screen. There are over 40 different designs, and they can’t all fit on a single page. Especially for responsive screens, it would be a nightmare to scroll through a single column of all these cards.


Following a Design System

Staying consistent within the company’s design patterns was another big priority, so I had to ensure that if there was a ‘Next’ button, it would be located at the bottom similar to other card action flows.


I considered the following options...

  1. Incremental ‘Load More’
  2. Internal scrollbar
  3. Modal pop-up
  4. Card as a ‘Next’ button (chosen solution)


Final Solution

In the end, I decided to have each card funtion as a ‘Next’ button directly taking users to the next step. So, clicking a card automatically leads to the next step with that design selected. This approach had the following benefits:


Familiar interaction

This direct selection is similar to online shopping experiences, where you browse through options before clicking one to be taken to a new detail page.


Reduced clicks

Don’t have to scroll down to or click an extra ‘Next’ button to continue. The card selection component was also not yet developed in the design system, but this was no longer necessary if the selected state was not visible.


Accessible and flexible

Reduces confusion since the Card Benefits link for Sports cards were an unexpected pause in screen readers when implemented as radio buttons. It also allows for category subheadings.



Reflection

Takeaways
  • The importance of communication within a cross-functional team. This was my first time designing in an industry setting, and a big part of my work was learning to clearly present design solutions and advocate for my decisions. I realized communicating my thought process was just as important as the design work itself.
  • Working in an agile environment and being open to new solutions and design directions along the way. During each sprint, I met with stakeholders to get continuous insights into areas like development and business interests that guided iterations.
  • Knowing what to prioritize to deliver a minimum viable product. We were working within a timeline, so I had to decide the most important features and focus on providing the greatest value given available resources.

In the future, I would...
  • Explore ways to integrate card benefits information within the wizard rather than on an external page link. This would help users stay within the experience and provide cleaner information flow.
  • Conduct user-testing to see how quickly and intuitively users are able to select a card and proceed throught the wizard steps.
  • Create mobile wireframes, so users can conveniently access responsive pages on their phones.
