Overview

ClassInsight is a project based on classroom-sensors to perform real-time data analysis on nonverbal behaviors. This technology collects anonymized data on location, body position, and gaze distribution over time. The goal is to help teachers make informed decisions about how to improve their teaching practices. This was an ongoing project within the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at CMU. I joined after initial classroom data was collected, and first round user interviews were conducted. My role was to interpret, analyze, and visualize this data through a dashboard design.



Problem

How might we visualize classroom data collected from ClassInsight sensors to encourage meaningful reflection and action from instructors?

Solution: Our team created a dashboard prototype to promote sense-making and teacher efficacy, or their confidence in their ability to guide students towards successful engagement and learning.



Process

Research Goals


To guide our research, we sought to understand...

  • What nonverbal data are teachers interested in?
  • How do teachers evaluate their own nonverbal performance?



Project Timeline


Thematic Analysis

We performed thematic analysis of user interviews conducted during the initial pilot study. In the studies, instructors gave their reactions to their nonverbal behavior data and recommended benchmarks from a classroom session. However, some teachers were resistant to change and felt certain behaviors were out of their control.

They were shown data such as their location distribution as well as where their gaze moved over time during a class session. Participants were made up of teaching instructors, tenured faculty, and teaching assistants at Carnegie Mellon. We coded about 25 interviews and generated codes based on recurring topics that surfaced instructors’ responses.



Graph visualizing the frequency of different codes and their interview quotes.


Interview Insights

Professors have mixed opinions about comparing data with their colleagues
  • Useful to have a benchmark, what’s realistic, guide goal setting
  • Prefer comparison to own data, different contexts/situations
  • Want to compare with the best instructor
Users wanted to see correlations between data
  • wanted to be able to draw connections between teacher and student data
Context was an important factor to evaluate performance
  • Individuality of the instructor’s situation and teaching style
  • Nonverbals can mean positive or negative engagement in various contexts
  • Annotations to see context
Many unsure how to set a good goal
  • Are other instructors’ averages a good metric
  • Precision of the data, numerical vs qualitative goals
  • Most advice was based on previous experience, but what do new instructors do?


Dashboard Visualizations

Based off these interview insights, we created a series of dashboard visualizations for a prototype to further test with teachers and get feedback. Our goal was to present the information in a motivating and understandable way. We incorporated features to encourage teachers to improve their teaching behaviors, such as the ability to set benchmarks as well as progress tracking over time.



Co-Design Interview Insights

For our second round of instructor interviews, we decided separated the different dashboard components to measure reactions to the graphs separately. We were able to get a better understanding of what it is teachers are really looking for to get effective feedback.


New Design Opportunities
  • Drop down to filter design categories and reduce clicks
  • Text describing sports cards’ special benefits links
  • More accessible back and next button
  • Present critical information earlier in the process


Lo-Fidelity Wireframes

After our brainstorming session, I got to work creating lo-fidelity wireframes incorporating initial ideas for the change card design flow. I wanted to create screens that made each step visually clear and simple while still communicating necessary information.



Heatmap of Nonverbals
  • Gaze was the measure of most interest, other data measures of interest: facial expression, performance in class activity, verbal data (tone or volume of voice)
  • Hand-raises are not viewed as a reliable measure of engagement, students might be engaged but not have questions, depends on class size
  • The importance of an intuitive color key, as color could be confusing with the use of blue in multiple areas although not correlated


Goal-Setting Graphs
  • Users want actionable suggestions
  • Teachers want the ability to customize the data towards their specific needs/classroom as well as a way to add annotations to understand context for the data
  • Confusion around terminology, need to define unclear words: diversity, balance, engagement
  • Teachers want to see visual relationships in the chart because they want to see the impact of their behavior on other measures


Reflection

Takeaways
  • You are not your user, although our research team was familiar with terminology we were using in our studies such as immediacy, location diversity, gaze balance, to users we were testing with these words had unclear or different meanings to each person. It’s important to consider designs not from our own perspective but be able to get outside opinions from our actual users. It would be helpful to use more everyday language on the dashboard to label data. (definition of the measures were unclear)
  • Consider everything as intentional, designs, although there was no meaning behind the order the graphs were presented, users often assumed that teacher data was correlated to the student data graph below
In the future, I would...
  • Create a more intuitive dashboard with emphasis on data of most interest supported by the user interviews.
  • Add features to focus on the “big picture” measurements to help teachers identify patterns.
