Overview

During my research internship at the Design Lab, I designed for Prototeams, a webapp facilitating small group interactions. Often in large settings such as classrooms or hackathons, it can be difficult and intimidating to find teammates. I worked with two developers to refine the app interface. This was an ongoing project, and I joined in the middle of development to build on designs.

Read more about our work in our CSCW research publication here.



Problem

How can we facilitate team formation in a large crowd?

Solution: ProtoTeams is a system designed to help people meet potential teammates through a series of short, small group activities. ProtoTeams eases the process of finding teams by helping users meet potential members and "prototype" teams before choosing members to work with.



How it works

ProtoTeams is divided into 3 components: a participant mobile interface, a shared display, and a host dashboard. The shared display is displayed or projected on a large screen and is controlled by the host. Each session has a certain number of rounds, each containing a number of activities, Sessions can be customized (group size, number of rounds, types of activities) by the host through the dashboard.




1. Join Session

Join the session by entering the unique session code generated on the shared display.




2. Find Your Group

Form groups by finding others with the same icon on their screen.




3. Group Activity

Participate in group activities and discussion questions. Timer in the top corner as well as Team member drop down menu.




4. Member Reflection

After each round, assess how well you got to know each member. If there are more rounds left, repeat group formation with a new set of people.

User Testing

We tested the new implementation in an undergraduate project-based design course at UCSD where students needed to find teammates for a quarter-long project. We conducted three pilot studies with the new tool during the first few weeks of class. Quantitative data (timing, member ratings) was collected through the app, and qualitative data (feedback, impressions) was collected through a post-session survey and user interviews



User Feedback

Observed during sessions: Students forming teams often raised screens up to display their shape icon to the rest of their team. Active engagement and discussion

Positive
  • Like the structured and communicative style of the questions Open ended questions
  • Diversity in meeting other people
  • Broke the ice, got to know people they normally would not have talked to
  • Better understanding of others’ skill sets
Suggested Impovements
  • Very short interactions, might not be enough time to get to know people
  • Difficulty discerning certain icons
  • No transition when switching teams
  • Everyone clustered in the center

Design Changes

Following the user testing sessions and feedback, we updated the app accordingly with the following design changes:

  • Increased time for group activities
  • Updated shape icons to appear more vibrant and with distinctive colors
  • Added transition screen when it is time to leave group


Team Confirmation

An issue that arose was trying to find a method for confirming group members had physically located each other. Previously, individuals could simply press a button to confirm their team was found, regardless if this was true or not. We considered various methods and eventually devided to assign each member an individual number of oranges and require the group sum to be entered as a means to ensure everyone was located.

Reflection

Takeaways
  • User testing is important. Testing in real classrooms was a very different experience than testing within our lab. New problems arose within lecture halls such as space arrangements and crowding.
  • Design is a lot of problem solving. I realized it’s important to get creative and be open to various possibilities when exploring visual solutions
  • Iterate! There’s always new features to be improved upon and having consistent iterations and sources of feedback really helped this project evolve.

In the future, I would...
  • Design the teacher dashboard to customize their own sessions to choose activites and number of rounds
  • Create a smoother onboarding process, so new instructors can easily facilitate their own sessions through the app
